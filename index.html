Scriptural Foundation

The Proof of the Ministry

On what grounds does the name stand?

Uri derelicti sumus

We are left to burn · Lamentations 5:20–22

The cry of Lamentations is not a cry of abandonment answered by silence — it is a cry preserved in sacred canon, which is itself the answer. The very fact that this anguish was recorded, copied, and carried through centuries of transmission testifies that it was heard. The flame that refines does not destroy what is precious; it reveals it.

Lamentations 5:20 — "Why dost thou forget us for ever, and forsake us so long time?"

Nonne cor nostrum ardens erat

Was not our heart burning within us · Luke 24:32

The disciples on the road to Emmaus did not recognize what was present until the moment of departure. Looking back, they identified the burning — not as destruction but as recognition. The ministry of Uri Derelicti stands on this distinction: the experience of burning is not evidence of God's absence but may be the most intimate sign of His presence, unrecognized in the moment.

Luke 24:32 — "Did not our heart burn within us, while he talked with us by the way?"

Ignis qui non comburit

The fire that does not consume · Exodus 3:2

The burning bush presented to Moses a paradox that the Scriptures do not resolve away — it burns, it is not consumed. This is the paradigm for the ministry's understanding of affliction and conscience: the soul that burns with longing or remorse is not being annihilated. Something holy is present in the flame. The proper response is not to flee it but, as Moses did, to turn aside and see.

Exodus 3:2 — "The bush burned with fire, and the bush was not consumed."
The Ministry

About Uri Derelicti

Uri Derelicti is a religious ministry organized as a nonprofit corporation under the laws of South Carolina. Its purpose is the study and proclamation of scriptural truth concerning conscience, desire, and the mercy of God available to the sincere seeker.

The name is drawn from the language of Lamentations — uri derelicti, "left to burn" — not as a statement of despair but as an honest acknowledgment of the human condition before God. The ministry holds that this condition, rightly understood, is the beginning of wisdom rather than the end of hope.

The ministry's work includes scriptural study, publication, and outreach to those navigating the difficult terrain of conscience and moral failure. It operates without denominational affiliation and draws from the breadth of the scriptural witness.

"The fire that does not destroy is the one that purifies. What is left burning may be precisely what is most worth keeping."
— Uri Derelicti
